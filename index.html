What research becomes

Research should resolve into record, extract, and verification.

GDER is not meant to stop at a list. The research layer is there to support a governed public record system with a canonical record, portable extract, and a verification path back to source.

01 Record

The reviewed public record becomes the canonical inspection surface.

02 Extract

Historical and certified outputs make the record portable without making it fuzzy.

03 Verify

A relying party should be able to check whether an extract still resolves back to the record.

What each entry should make legible

Enough structure for a counterparty to make a serious judgment.

A good GDER entry should quickly clarify the wrapper, jurisdiction, governance anchor, and basis for why the entry exists at all. That is the difference between a directory and an inspection surface.

Legal wrapper

The legal or quasi-legal structure behind the entity.

Jurisdiction

Where the structure sits and what legal context it operates in.

Governance anchor

The official source, forum, vote, or governance material that supports the entry.

Basis

The short explanation for why the entry qualifies for structured inclusion.

Secondary action

When an entity should be added, start the review path.

Review comes after someone understands the product. If an entity should move toward a governed public record, the site should make the next step obvious without turning the homepage into a submission form first.
