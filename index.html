How GDER works

From research to reviewed public record.

GDER becomes useful when research turns into an inspectable public record with history, portable extracts, and a verification path that lets others rely on what they see.

01
Reviewed public record

The reviewed record becomes the canonical inspection surface for the entity.

02
Portable extract

Certified or historical extracts let the record travel into diligence, onboarding, and counterparty workflows.

03
Verification path

A relying party should be able to confirm that an extract still resolves back to the underlying record.

What a record should show

The fields that make an entity intelligible.

A useful GDER record should make the wrapper, jurisdiction, governance, and control surface visible enough for serious counterparties to orient quickly.

Legal wrapper

The legal or quasi-legal structure the entity actually uses.

Jurisdiction

Where the structure sits and which legal context applies.

Governance anchor

The official docs, forum, vote, or constitutional source behind the entity.

Control surface

The treasury or operating-control evidence that helps show how the entity works in practice.
