Record system

Research should mature into record, extract, and verification.

The research set is the starting layer, not the endpoint. The actual GDER product is a governed public record system: one canonical record, portable extracts, and a verification path that resolves back to source.

01
Canonical record

The reviewed public record becomes the main inspection surface.

02
Portable extract

03
Verification path

A relying party should be able to check whether an extract still resolves back to the underlying record.

Inspection model

Enough structure for a counterparty to make a serious judgment.

A good GDER entry should quickly clarify the wrapper, jurisdiction, governance anchor, and basis for why the entity qualifies for structured inclusion.

Legal wrapper

The legal or quasi-legal structure behind the entity.

Jurisdiction

The relevant legal context and where the structure sits.

Governance anchor

The source, forum, proposal, vote, or official material supporting the entry.

Basis

The concise explanation for why the entry belongs in the structured set.

Secondary action
